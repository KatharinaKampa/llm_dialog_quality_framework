Manual Evaluation Log — BAD Diaogs (First Generation Run)

Evaluator: Katharina Kampa
Purpose: - Manual quality review after automated pipeline check
         - Identification of candidates for Gold / IMC dialogs

Review criteria applied to each dialog:
  - Is the dialog linguistically comprehensible (despite intentional poor communication)?
  - Does the LLM evaluation show a clear orientation? (e.g. Overall 1/2, not 4; not 6 or 7)
  - Is the evaluation stable? (no contradictory subscales, no noise)
  - Does the dialog stay within the intended recipe/task frame?
  - Are the intended communication violations clearly present?

Regenerate criteria (R-codes):
  R1_OUT_OF_SCOPE        : Not within the intended recipe/task frame
  R2_FORMAT_BROKEN       : Speaker/turns broken, unreadable
  R3_CONTENT_ERROR       : Hard content errors (wrong values, hallucinations)
  R4_TOO_SHORT_REPETITIVE: Too short / strong repetitions
  R5_MEASUREMENT_ARTIFACT: Grams/percent/serving formats visible (not intended)
  R6_CONSTRAINT_VIOLATION: Violates study plan / stimulus rules
  R7_OTHER               : Other (with notes)

Gold dialog criteria (BAD):
  - Very clear BAD dialogs + LLM Overall 1/2 and all subscales low
  - Used to compare human ratings with LLM-based evaluations

IMC dialog criteria (BAD):
  - Very clear BAD dialogs with highly unambiguous expected ratings
  - Used to detect inattentive or non-compliant participants
  - Marked with * if confirmed as IMC in second batch

Note: Gold and IMC dialogs were selected after main stimulus selection to avoid biasing the overall stimulus distribution.
